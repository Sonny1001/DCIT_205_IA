Information Security and Ethical Hacking

Here are some of the topics you will come across when you enroll into our Web Development programme:

  1. Information Security Fundamentals

    Information security is the practice of protecting information from unauthorized access, disclosure, disruption, modification, or destruction. It involves implementing measures to ensure the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of data.

  2. Penetration Testing

    Penetration testing, or ethical hacking, involves simulating real-world attacks on a system to identify and exploit vulnerabilities. This proactive approach helps organizations understand their security weaknesses and take corrective actions.

  3. Firewalls

    Implement firewalls to monitor and control incoming and outgoing network traffic. Firewalls act as a barrier between a trusted internal network and untrusted external networks, preventing unauthorized access and protecting against cyber threats.

  4. Encryption

    Use strong encryption algorithms to protect sensitive data during transmission and storage. This includes encrypting communications with protocols like HTTPS and ensuring that stored data is properly encrypted.

  5. Incident Response and Digital Forensics

    Having a well-defined incident response plan is crucial for quickly and effectively responding to security incidents. Digital forensics involves analyzing and recovering information from digital devices to investigate and prevent future incidents.

  6. Blockchain Security

    As blockchain technology becomes more prevalent, understanding its security implications is crucial. This includes securing smart contracts, protecting against 51% attacks, and ensuring the integrity of distributed ledger systems.

  7. Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

    Implement MFA to add an extra layer of security beyond traditional username and password authentication. This typically involves using a combination of something you know (password) and something you have (e.g., a mobile device or security token).

  8. Network Segmentation

    Divide networks into segments to restrict access to sensitive information. This limits the potential impact of a security breach and prevents lateral movement by attackers within the network.

  9. Honeypots

    Deploy honeypots, which are decoy systems designed to lure attackers. By analyzing the techniques used by attackers on honeypots, organizations can better understand potential threats and strengthen their defenses.

  10. Endpoint Security

    Ensure the security of individual devices (endpoints) through measures such as antivirus software, endpoint detection and response (EDR) solutions, and device management policies.
